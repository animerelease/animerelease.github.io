# The Crunchyroll Problem

This site is a neutral dataset: every English anime disc release we can find, machine-readable, updated daily. This page is not neutral. It's the reason the site exists.

## One company bought the market

Over nine years, Sony assembled nearly every layer of English-language anime distribution into one company:

- **2017** — Sony Pictures Television acquires a controlling stake in [Funimation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funimation), the largest US anime distributor.
- **2019** — Aniplex (Sony) buys [Madman Anime Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crunchyroll_Store_Australia), Australia's anime distributor, for A$35M.
- **2021** — Sony buys Crunchyroll from AT&T for [$1.175 billion](https://variety.com/2022/digital/news/crunchyroll-adds-funimation-anime-sony-1235192876/). The two largest anime streaming services are now one company.
- **2022** — Funimation, Wakanim and VRV are folded into Crunchyroll. In August, Crunchyroll [acquires Right Stuf](https://anitrendz.net/news/2022/08/04/crunchyroll-purchases-right-stuf/), the beloved 35-year-old anime retailer, promising "to enrich the shopping experience for the anime community."
- **2023** — Right Stuf is [shut down and migrated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_Stuf) into the Crunchyroll Store (October). Madman's anime store becomes Crunchyroll Store Australia (January).
- **2024** — Sony [shuts down Funimation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Funimation) (April 2). Digital copies that customers "owned" through Funimation are wiped, not migrated. Remember that one whenever someone says you don't need the disc.
- **2025–2026** — The Crunchyroll Store quietly delists hundreds of Blu-rays and manga sets. Backorders are [cancelled en masse](https://finalweapon.net/2026/05/05/crunchyroll-is-turning-its-back-on-anime-physical-media/); entire seasons of major series have zero listings while the same store sells hoodies. New CR-distributed sets creep from the $40–50 that was normal a few years ago to $70–80 MSRP.
- **July 2026** — Crunchyroll announces the store is going [members-only](https://finalweapon.net/2026/07/14/crunchyroll-store-moving-to-collectibles-curated-drops-and-limited-release-products-after-delisting-hundreds-of-manga-blu-ray-sets/): starting August, you need a Mega Fan or Ultimate Fan subscription to buy anything, and the store pivots to "collectibles, curated drops, and limited-release products."

Follow the arc: buy the competition, absorb it, promise continuity, then dismantle the parts that served collectors. A store you must subscribe to browse is not a store. It's a loyalty program with a paywall.

## Why this matters even if you only buy US discs

This mostly reads as a US story — Right Stuf was the US storefront — but the same playbook ran in three markets. Australia lost Madman's anime arm to the same owner. The UK's Funimation operation became Crunchyroll UK. When one company controls streaming, licensing (Aniplex), *and* the dominant storefronts across the English-speaking world, "we've deprioritized physical media" isn't a business decision you can shop around. It's the market deciding for you.

And no, Amazon isn't the answer. It's currently the fallback of necessity for Crunchyroll and Aniplex discs — and genuinely useful for out-of-print hunting — but it's a packaging lottery run by another giant, not an anime retailer. Relying on it is how we got here.

## Where your money actually helps

The good news: the distributors who still treat discs as the product, not merch-adjacent inventory, are easy to find — and most of them are exactly who this site tracks.

**North America**
- [Discotek Media](https://mediaocd.com/) (via MediaOCD) — the gold standard for catalog rescues and restorations
- [Sentai Filmworks](https://www.sentaifilmworks.com/) — direct store, deep catalog
- [GKIDS](https://gruv.com/) (via GRUV) — Ghibli, films, and now TV series at half Crunchyroll's asking price, with actual bonus features
- [AnimEigo](https://mediaocd.com/) and [Media Blasters](https://mediaocd.com/) — classics, also via MediaOCD
- [Anime Corner Store](https://www.animecornerstore.com/) — independent retailer, 25+ years running

**United Kingdom & Ireland**
- [Anime Limited](https://alltheanime.com/) — collector editions done right
- [MVM Entertainment](https://www.mvm-films.com/) — independent since 1993

**Australia & New Zealand**
- [Sugoi Co](https://sugoi.shop/) — founded by ex-Madman anime staff after the buyout; AU/NZ's independent distributor

Every release from these distributors that we can index is in [the tracker](/), with dates, formats, regions and identifiers — as open data anyone can download, archive, and build on. No affiliate links, no ads, no subscription. That's the point.
